Resistors of resistance 100 Ω each are connected in parallel in an electric circuit. Then the number of resistors have to be connected to obtain 25 Ω as equivalent resistance is

1 mark Easy
For parallel resistors: 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + ... = 1/Rₑq
Since all resistors are equal (100Ω): n/100 = 1/25
Where n is the number of resistors
n = 100/25 = 4 resistors
Therefore, option (D) 4 is correct.

Is it possible to connect a refrigerator rated 1320W to a domestic circuit of 220 V with an electric fuse rated 5 A? Clarify the reason for your answer.

2 marks Medium
Step 1: Let's use the power equation to find current
Given: Power (P) = 1320W, Voltage (V) = 220V
Using P = V × I
1320 = 220 × I
I = 1320/220 = 6 amperes
Step 2: Compare with fuse rating of 5A
Since 6A > 5A, the current required exceeds the fuse rating
Therefore, it's not possible to connect the refrigerator as it will blow the fuse

It is advantageous to connect electric devices in parallel instead of connecting them in series. Why?

3 marks Medium

OR

According to Joule's law of heating, mention the factors on which heat produced in a resistor depends. According to this law write the formula used to calculate the heat produced.

Part 1: Advantages of parallel connection:
1. The parallel circuit divides current through the electrical gadgets based on their resistance
2. When one component fails, other components continue to work
3. Each electrical appliance can be operated independently
4. Devices get the required current based on their individual resistance
OR
Joule's Law of Heating:
1. Heat is directly proportional to square of current (I²)
2. Heat is directly proportional to resistance (R)
3. Heat is directly proportional to time (t)
4. Formula: H = I²Rt

The correct formula that shows the relationship between potential difference, electric current and resistance in an electric circuit is

1 mark Easy
Option (D) R = V/I is correct
This is Ohm's law which states that:
- The ratio of potential difference (V) to current (I) is constant
- This constant is called resistance (R)
- Therefore, R = V/I is the correct relationship
